Lawful Disposal
Destroy eligible records correctly and document everything
0%
▼
Legal Requirement: No public record may be destroyed without a signed RM-3 on file. Destroying records without this violates Virginia Code § 42.1-85 and exposes the town to liability.
RM-3 Destruction Process
Download RM-3 Certificate of Records Destruction from lva.virginia.gov
RM-3
Complete RM-3 with record series, box numbers, date ranges, and retention schedule reference
Example: "Audio Recording, Council Meeting Nov 4 2024 — minutes adopted Dec 2 2024 — GS-19"
Complete
Obtain signatures of Records Officer (Clerk) and approving official (Mayor)
Both signatures required before any destruction begins.
Sign
Shred or securely destroy eligible paper records
Shred
Destroy eligible tapes — cut tape or degauss media
Tapes
File completed RM-3 permanently in town office records
RM-3 is itself a permanent record — your legal proof of lawful disposal.
File
Tape-to-Digital Conversion
Digitize audio from tapes without confirmed adopted minutes before destroying
Play tape near mic captured by Zoom (record to cloud) or USB cassette converter (~$30).
Digitize
Transcribe audio using Otter.ai, Maestra, or Google Docs Voice Typing (free)
Clean up transcript, format as official minutes, present for council adoption vote.
Transcribe
After council adoption vote — delete MP3 and complete RM-3 for original tape
Cleanup
