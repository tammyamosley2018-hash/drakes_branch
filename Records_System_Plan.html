About This Plan

The Town of Drakes Branch holds decades of municipal records — many dating back to the 1960s and earlier — currently stored in over 100 boxes. This plan provides a step-by-step guide to properly assess, retain, transfer, dispose of, and digitize those records in full compliance with Virginia law and the Library of Virginia's Local Government Records Program.

Check off each task as it is completed. Progress is tracked automatically. This document may be used as both a working SOP and a presentation tool for town leadership.

Triage & Assessment

Goal: Walk through all boxes and create a simple inventory before reorganizing anything. Do not discard or move records yet. Understanding what exists is the foundation of the entire project.

Retention Decisions
Categorize every record: Keep, Transfer, or Destroy
0%
▼
Important: No records may be destroyed without completing the RM-3 Certificate of Destruction and obtaining the mayor's signature. Virginia law requires this documentation. Keep all RM-3 forms permanently on file.

Lawful Disposal
Destroy eligible records correctly and document everything
0%
▼
Legal Requirement: No public record may be destroyed without a signed RM-3 Certificate of Records Destruction on file. Destroying records without this documentation violates Virginia Code § 42.1-85 and can expose the town to liability. The completed RM-3 must be kept permanently.
RM-3 Destruction Process
Download the RM-3 Certificate of Records Destruction form from lva.virginia.gov
RM-3 Form
Complete RM-3 listing each record series, box numbers, date ranges, and retention schedule reference
Example entry: "Audio Recording, Town Council Meeting, November 4 2024 — used to produce official minutes adopted December 2 2024 — GS-19"
Complete
Obtain signatures of the Records Officer (Town Clerk) and approving official (Mayor) on RM-3
Both signatures are required before any destruction can take place.
Sign
Physically destroy eligible paper records — shredding is the standard method for confidential documents
For large volumes, a shredding service can be brought in. Non-confidential records may be recycled.
Shred
Destroy eligible tape recordings — cut tape or degauss (magnetically erase) the media
Physical cutting is sufficient. Document method used on RM-3.
Tapes
File completed RM-3 form permanently in the town office records
The RM-3 itself is a permanent record. It is your legal proof of lawful disposal.
File RM-3
Tape-to-Digital Conversion (Before Destruction)
For tapes without confirmed adopted minutes — digitize audio before destroying
Play tape near a microphone captured by Zoom (record to cloud) or use a USB cassette converter (~$30). Save MP3 temporarily to Google Drive.
Digitize
Transcribe audio to Google Doc using Otter.ai, Maestra, or Google Docs Voice Typing (free)
Clean up transcript, format as official minutes, present to council for adoption vote.
Transcribe
Once minutes are officially adopted by council vote — delete the MP3 and complete RM-3 for the original tape
The adopted Google Doc minutes are the official permanent record. The audio file is a working draft only.

Digital Filing System
Build the system that keeps Drakes Branch organized going forward
0%
▼
Goal: Once the backlog is cleared, build a simple, maintainable digital system so no record ever ends up in a box again. This system is designed to run on Google Drive's free plan and requires no technical expertise to maintain.
Google Drive Folder Structure
Create master folder: "Town of Drakes Branch — Official Records"
This is the root folder. All other folders live inside it.
Drive
Create six subfolders: 01-Council Records | 02-Financial | 03-Utilities | 04-Personnel | 05-Correspondence | 06-Permits & Land
Numbering keeps folders in logical order. Color-code each folder for fast visual identification.
Folders
Inside each subfolder, create year folders: 2024, 2025, 2026 etc.
Example path: Council Records → 2025 → Minutes_2025-03-04_Adopted.pdf
Year Folders
Establish a file naming convention and post it as a written SOP in the office
Format: [Category]_[YYYY-MM-DD]_[Description] — Example: Minutes_2025-04-01_Adopted.pdf
SOP
Scanning & Migration
Prioritize scanning all permanent records first — Council Minutes, Ordinances, Resolutions, Deeds
These are the most critical. Scan to PDF and upload to the correct folder immediately.
Scan First
Scan remaining retained records by category — Financial, Utilities, Personnel, Correspondence
Work through boxes in batches. Scan, name, upload, file physical copy or destroy per schedule.
Scan
Set up a physical color-coded hanging folder system for any remaining paper records
Match folder colors to Google Drive categories. Anyone in the office can find anything within 30 seconds.
Physical
Ongoing Intake Process
Establish a same-day filing rule — every new document gets logged, scanned, and filed the day it arrives
This is the most important SOP. The backlog exists because intake was never systematized.
SOP
Set up Zoom or Otter.ai to automatically transcribe all future council meetings going forward
Record meeting via Zoom, export transcript, clean up, present for adoption. Eliminates tape recorder dependency permanently.
Zoom AI
Schedule an annual records review each January — purge what has met its retention period, complete RM-3
A one-hour annual review prevents the next 60-year backlog from ever accumulating.
Annual
Monitor Google Drive storage — stay within free 15GB limit by keeping only active records in Drive; transfer completed year archives to LVA or physical storage as needed
